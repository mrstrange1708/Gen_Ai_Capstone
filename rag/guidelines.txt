1. Patients with previous no-shows are at high risk of missing future appointments; mandate a human verification call 48 hours prior.
2. SMS reminders sent exactly 24 hours before appointments reduce no-show rates by up to 30%.
3. Long lead times (over 21 days) between booking and appointment dramatically increase no-show probability; schedule midway touchpoints to confirm.
4. Offering flexible, self-serve rescheduling links in confirmation texts improves patient attendance.
5. Teleconsultation can reduce missed appointments for high-risk patients facing transportation or distance barriers (over 15km).
6. Patients with multiple chronic conditions (e.g., diabetes and hypertension) should be evaluated for transportation assistance or remote monitoring.
7. Uninsured patients face financial barriers; provide billing transparency or financial counseling before the appointment.
8. Unemployed patients have higher no-show rates; offer flexible hours or subsidized transportation if available.
9. Elderly patients (65+) may struggle with digital reminders; prioritize phone outreach or caregiver coordination.
10. Young adults (18-30) respond best to asynchronous communication like SMS or WhatsApp rather than voice calls.
11. Appointments scheduled for early mornings on Mondays have higher missed rates; send a Sunday evening reminder.
12. Inclement weather conditions (e.g., heavy rain) disproportionately affect patients traveling long distances; proactively offer telehealth switches.
13. First-time clinic patients lack routine; provide clear wayfinding, parking instructions, and arrival expectations.
14. Patients with a 'high travel burden' (>45 minutes) should be prioritized for block scheduling to minimize the number of required trips.
15. If a patient misses two consecutive appointments, flag for a social worker outreach to assess socioeconomic barriers.
16. Sending an email reminder 3 days prior, followed by an SMS 1 day prior, is the optimal cadence for specialty departments (e.g., Cardiology, Orthopedics).
17. Pediatric appointments often rely on parent availability; ensure reminders allow calendar integration for working parents.
18. Same-day appointments have the lowest no-show rates; utilize a waitlist system to fill sudden cancellations.
19. Patients who frequently cancel and reschedule should be required to speak directly with triage before rebooking.
20. Overbooking by 10-15% is permissible only in slots historically known for high no-shows (e.g., Friday afternoons).
21. If a patient is flagged as "High Risk," assigning a dedicated care coordinator for a 5-minute pre-visit prep call reduces no-shows and improves satisfaction.
22. Evening appointment slots have higher attendance rates for blue-collar employed patients; limit daytime scheduling for this demographic.
23. "No-show rate thresholds": Departments exceeding a 15% no-show rate must implement mandatory SMS confirmation.
24. Language or literacy barriers can cause confusion; ensure reminders are sent in the patient's primary language.
25. Patients with a history of missed appointments should be offered slots later in the day, as morning logistics are common failure points.
